### **OOZ3: Slilab** 🟢 🤖
### [GitHub Repository](https://github.com/KevinMichael4441/Games-Fleadh)

I was the **team captain and lead programmer** for our 'Game Studio' entry in Games Fleadh 2026. We ended up winning the **"Best Game Built using Software Libraries"** title. The game can be compiled for and played on Windows,
Linux, web and the R36S/r36xx handheld deviceS (binaries from ARM cross-compilation).


My main contribution to the project was creating a soft-body player that can squish and stretch, giving it a slimy feel. Additionally, as team captain, I was in charge of managing my team by 
ensuring we meet deadlines, maintaining correspondence between my teammates and our academic mentor, and helping my team with any debugging issues and merge conflicts. 

Over the course of the 6 weeks that we did the project, I learnt a mountain of knowledge - from setting up dev containers to solving deltaTime issues specific to R36S - pretty much everything I did was new to me and a learning journey.

### **BigMack for RoboCode**
### [Source Code](https://github.com/KevinMichael4441/roboCodeProgress.git)
For the national RoboCode competition at Games Fleadh 2025, I made a battle tank called BigMack with two teammates representing my university and campus - SETU Carlow. From the group stage to finals, we won without losing a single match.

I was in charge of writing code for firing. I implemented 3 types of firing techniques, one of which was a linear targeting algorithm. I made the two other algorithms by hand after 
thoroughly studying the rules of RoboCode and how inheritance worked in Java (Inheritance was a new concept to me at the time). 

The second algorithm was a sort of 'average-shot' where BigMack would calculate the average position of the opponent from the last n ticks. 
This algorithm was made to target opponents moving in a circular motion. The final algorithm was simply firing where the opponent currently is. Although extremely simple in concept,
this algorithm was made after extensive testing in trying to find a balance between the two previously mentioned attack strategies.
