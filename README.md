### **OOZ3: Slilab** 🟢
### [GitHub Repository](https://github.com/KevinMichael4441/Games-Fleadh)

I was the **team captain and lead programmer** for our 'Game Studio' entry in Games Fleadh 2026. We ended up winning the **"Best Game Built using Software Libraries"** title. The game can be compiled for and played on **Windows,
Linux, web and the R36S/R36XX handheld devices (binaries from ARM cross-compilation)**.


My main contribution to the project was creating a soft-body player that can squish and stretch, giving it an organic feel. Additionally, as team captain, I was in charge of managing my team by ensuring we meet deadlines, maintaining correspondence between my teammates and our academic mentor, and helping my team with any debugging issues and merge conflicts. 

Over the course of the 6 weeks that we did the project, I learnt a mountain of knowledge - from setting up dev containers to solving deltaTime issues specific to R36S - pretty much everything I did was new to me and a learning journey.

### **BigMack for RoboCode** 🤖
### [Source Code](https://github.com/KevinMichael4441/roboCodeProgress.git)
For the national RoboCode competition at Games Fleadh 2025, I made a battle tank called BigMack with two teammates representing my university and campus - SETU Carlow. From the group stage to finals, we did not lose a single match.

I was in charge of writing code for firing. I implemented 3 types of firing techniques, one of which was a linear targeting algorithm. I made the two other algorithms by hand after thoroughly studying the rules of RoboCode and how inheritance worked in **Java** (Inheritance was a new concept to me at the time). 

The second algorithm was a sort of 'average-shot' where BigMack would calculate the average position of the opponent from the last n ticks. 
This algorithm was made to target opponents moving in a circular motion. The final algorithm was simply firing where the opponent currently is. Although extremely simple in concept, this algorithm was made after extensive testing in trying to find a balance between the two previously mentioned attack strategies.

### **Karak Rush** 🍵

**I partnered with the Dubai Department of Economy and Tourism** to create a prototype showcasing Dubai culture. I was in a team of two for this project. We were invited to a 2-week bootcamp where we learnt to use **Maya and Unity**. This was our first time using these tools. We were taught by industry professionals working in Maysalward, a game development company in the Middle East. I focused on making models in Maya and implementing them in the game. This includes the character and his animations. After the two-week bootcamp, the prototype was **made into a mobile application**. It was then **exhibited at the Dubai Games Expo 2024**. Here, we were able to talk and listen to investors and other game-development-related figures in the Middle East. 

 The repository for it cannot be shared as it is private and not owned by me.
